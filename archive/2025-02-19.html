Jazz Pioneers

February 19 - February 25, 2025

About This Selection

Eastern Sounds is a studio album by the jazz multi-instrumentalist and composer Yusef Lateef. It was released in April 1962 through Prestige Records.

This groundbreaking album reflects Lateef's exploration of Eastern musical traditions, combining them with Western jazz idioms to create a unique sonic landscape. His innovative approach to composition and instrumentation established him as one of the pioneers in world music fusion, decades before the term was widely used.

Album Notes

On Eastern Sounds, Lateef showcases his versatility by playing not only tenor saxophone but also the oboe, flute, and various Eastern instruments like the rabat and shanai. The album's exotic scales, modal explorations, and unusual time signatures opened new pathways for jazz musicians of the era.

The album features Lateef alongside pianist Barry Harris, drummer Lex Humphries, and bassist Ernie Farrow, who also plays rabat on several tracks. The ensemble creates a meditative, contemplative atmosphere that transports listeners to distant lands while maintaining a strong connection to the jazz tradition.
